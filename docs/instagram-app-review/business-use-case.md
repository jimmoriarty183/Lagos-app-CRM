## App Overview (если попросят описать продукт целиком)

```
Ordo AI Sales Manager is a SaaS tool that helps small e-commerce businesses
automate customer support and sales conversations on Instagram Direct.

When a customer messages an Ordo-connected Instagram Business account, our
service receives the message via Instagram Webhooks, looks up the business's
product catalog (provided by the merchant as a Google Sheet), generates a
contextual reply using Google's Gemini language model, and sends the reply
back to the customer through the Instagram Graph API.

Typical use cases include answering product questions ("does this come in
black?"), recommending items based on customer needs ("I want headphones for
running, budget under $100"), and sharing payment links to close sales
("here's the link to buy the Sonic Sport V2: …").

The merchant retains full control: they update their catalog spreadsheet to
change available products, prices, and descriptions, and the AI uses that
data as the only source of truth for recommendations.
```

## Permission: `instagram_business_basic`

### How will your app use this permission?

```
We use instagram_business_basic to read the connected Instagram Business
account's profile information (id, username, account_type) when the
business owner connects their account during onboarding. This lets us
verify the account is a Business or Creator account (required for
messaging) and display the connected handle in the Ordo dashboard so the
merchant can confirm they connected the right account.

We do not store any other profile data. Username is shown back to the
merchant only.
```

### Step-by-step example

```
1. Merchant signs up at ordo.uno and clicks "Connect Instagram"
2. Merchant authenticates via Instagram Login OAuth
3. Our backend calls GET /me?fields=id,username,account_type
4. We display the username in the Ordo settings page so the merchant
   confirms the right account is connected
5. We use the id (PSID) when sending replies via Graph API
```

## Permission: `instagram_business_manage_messages`

### How will your app use this permission?

```
This is the core permission for our service. We use
instagram_business_manage_messages to:

(1) Receive incoming Direct Messages from customers via the messages
    webhook field.
(2) Reply to those customers using the connected business account, by
    POSTing to /me/messages with the customer's PSID and the AI-generated
    reply text.

The bot only replies inside the standard 24-hour customer service window
opened by the customer's incoming message. We never send unsolicited
outbound messages, never send promotional broadcasts, and never message
users who have not first contacted the business.

Each reply is generated by Google's Gemini model from the customer's
message and the merchant's product catalog. The bot does not store
message content beyond what is required to log webhook deliveries
(retained 30 days for debugging, then deleted).
```

### Step-by-step example

```
1. Customer sends a DM to a connected Instagram Business account
2. Meta posts a webhook to https://ordo.uno/api/webhooks/instagram
3. Our handler verifies the X-Hub-Signature-256 HMAC against the app secret
4. Handler extracts sender.id and message.text from the payload
5. Handler loads the merchant's catalog from their Google Sheet
6. Handler asks Gemini for a contextual reply using the catalog as context
7. Handler POSTs the reply to /me/messages with recipient.id = sender.id
8. Customer receives the reply in their DM
```

### What types of users will see this feature?

```
End customers who have initiated a conversation with an Ordo-connected
Instagram Business account.

Business owners who connect their Instagram Business account to Ordo to
automate replies to their inbound DMs.
```

## Data Use Checkup (раздел в Submit форме)

### What data does your app collect?

```
- Instagram Business profile info (id, username, account_type) — for the
  connected account only, displayed back to the merchant
- Direct Message text — passed to Gemini for reply generation, logged for
  30 days for debugging then deleted
- Customer PSIDs — used to route replies, retained for the lifetime of
  the merchant's subscription
- Merchant's Google Sheet contents — fetched live on each message,
  cached for 60 seconds, never persisted
```

### How is data used?

```
Solely to generate contextual sales-assistant replies on behalf of the
business owner who installed our app. No data is sold, shared with
advertisers, or used to train AI models. Gemini API requests are sent
with explicit no-training metadata where Google supports it.
```

### How long is data retained?

```
- Webhook event logs: 30 days
- Customer PSIDs: until merchant cancels their Ordo subscription
- Gemini request/response: not stored beyond the lifetime of a single
  HTTP request
- Merchant's catalog data: not stored — fetched live from their Google
  Sheet on every message
```

### How can users delete their data?

```
Customers can email support@ordo.uno from the address tied to their
Instagram account, and we will delete all PSID-keyed records within 30
days, in accordance with our Data Deletion policy at
https://ordo.uno/data-deletion.

Merchants can revoke our app's access at any time from their Facebook
account settings under Apps and Websites; revocation immediately stops
all data flow.
```

## Tips for the Submission

1. **Don't use marketing language.** Meta reviewers want concrete technical descriptions, not "revolutionizing customer experience". State exactly which API endpoints you call and what you do with the response.

2. **Match permission justifications to actual API calls.** If you say you use `instagram_business_manage_messages` to read messages but the test API calls show only sending — they'll reject. Test calls and justification text must align.

3. **Test calls before Submit.** Open `test-api-calls.md` and complete every required call BEFORE clicking Submit. Forms with `0 of 1 API call(s) required` cannot be submitted.

4. **Screencast is mandatory.** They will reject without one. See `screencast-script.md` for the exact 60-second flow.

5. **Privacy and Data Deletion URLs must be accessible.** They WILL fetch them. See `privacy-checklist.md` for what must be on those pages.

6. **Business verification.** If the app is owned by a Business Manager, that BM must be verified before App Review. If unverified, expect a "Business Verification required" rejection. Submit business verification first, wait 3-5 days, then submit App Review.

7. **Allow 1-7 business days.** Average is 2-3 days. If rejected, address specific feedback and re-submit — usually approved on second try.
